Why This Structure Exists

Build Canada Homes was designed for buildings exactly like this.

Small-format, mission-aligned residential assets in growing-but-not-urban Ontario towns sit in a structural financing gap.

01

Too small for institutional debt

$22M total cost is below the threshold most senior-housing REITs and pension funds will underwrite as a single asset.

02

Too NOI-thin for conventional 30-year amort

Stabilized NOI of $1.14M can't service market-rate 30-year debt on the construction cost it requires.

03

Exactly the asset class BCH targets

Affordable housing for vulnerable populations, in mid-sized Ontario communities, with NFP alignment and municipal partnership.

Risks & Considerations

What we are still verifying.

BCH Eligibility

Program qualification not yet confirmed

BCH 40% forgivable requires NFP sponsor, deep affordability covenant, and program approval. Mitigation: structure NFP partnership early; engage CMHC and BCH at concept stage.

Total Project Cost

$22M is a working assumption

Detailed QS estimate required. Boutique senior living in small-market Ontario typically prices $750–$950/SF all-in. Mitigation: lock GMP procurement.

Forgiveness Mechanics

Forgivable timeline is program-dependent

Returns model assumes BCH forgivable portion remains forgiven through 5-yr hold. If forgiveness vests over 20 years, early sale may trigger partial repayment.

Lease-Up Pace

Year 1 BCH DCR is 0.91×

Lease-up reserve required. Y1 occupancy of 82% must be defended with referral pipeline (physicians, hospital discharge planners).

Affordability Covenant

BCH may require below-market set-aside

If 30%+ of units must be at affordable rates, blended rent of $4,580/mo will compress. Mitigation: model affordable variant with discount on a portion of suites.

Regulatory Path

RHRA licensing locks operating model

Licensed Independent-Supportive requires 2+ regulated services (meals + medication oversight). Mitigation: confirm path before construction docs.
