Risk & Mitigation

Every risk. Mapped, priced, and structurally mitigated.

Institutional capital requires a transparent risk framework. Below are the five principal risk categories for this transaction, each with a specific, contractual or structural mitigant.

Construction Cost Risk

Hard-cost escalation during build

Fluctuations in labour, steel, and concrete pricing during the 2028 build window.

Mitigation: Fixed-price contract with Cube Building Systems. Offsite prefab (CLT/CFS hybrid) removes ~60% of onsite variability and accelerates schedule.
Lease-Up Risk

Absorption velocity on delivery

Delivering 220 units in Q4 2029 without pre-leasing would pressure cash flow.

Mitigation: CMHC MLI 10-year rent guarantee mechanism + affordable units leased first via BCH waiting list. Market rents at $1,080/SF against $1,500/SF projected sell-out — substantial margin of safety.
Regulatory Risk

Zoning, rent control, or NFP rule changes

Ontario rental legislation or BCH program terms could shift post-close.

Mitigation: NFP structure + signed BCH funding agreement locks affordability in a federal framework. Zoning secured by Q1 2027 — before any capital call.
Interest Rate Risk

Rate environment at loan renewal

50-year amortization means multiple renewal windows across the hold.

Mitigation: CMHC MLI at 3.40% (5-yr term, 50-yr amort), 95% LTV — the lowest-cost institutional debt available in Canada. Lock available for 12 mo pre-funding.
Market Risk

Toronto rental softening

Broader GTA oversupply narrative, particularly in downtown core.

Mitigation: Lakeshore corridor outperforms GTA average by ~120 bps of annual rent growth. 501 streetcar + Mimico GO + planned Ontario Line stop at Park Lawn anchor transit access for decades.
Sponsor / Execution Risk

Alignment of operator, NFP, and LPs

First-of-kind structures require disciplined governance.

Mitigation: GP/LP waterfall; Integrity Tree Financial structuring; independent trustee over NFP 40% distribution; quarterly reporting; annual independent audit.
