Executive Summary

A C$22M project. Two financing paths. One stronger structure.

Heal House Listowel is a 49-unit boutique senior independent living residence at 469 Main St W, structured as a non-profit-partnered, BCH-financed asset.

Without the BCH program, the boutique-scale economics are materially tighter. With it, the same operating pro forma generates a 2.18× DCR by Year 5 on a 5% equity contribution — and a financeable, replicable Ontario prototype.

The Capital Question

At $22M total cost, can a 49-unit boutique senior building service conventional 30-year debt?

Verified Inputs

The pro forma is set.

Year 5 NOI of $1.14M at 30% margin and 95% occupancy is achievable. Suite mix, unit count, and revenue are verified from architectural plans and operator pro forma.

The Arithmetic Problem

A small building has small NOI.

$1.14M at stabilization cannot service a $16.5M conventional loan amortized over 30 years at 5.99%. Annual debt service: $1.19M. DCR: 0.96×.

The BCH Answer

A different stack.

40% forgivable, 50% MLI Select 50yr @ 4.15%, 5% owner, 5% municipality. Same NOI, new debt service: $522K. DCR: 2.18×.

The BCH Structure · How Cash Flow Splits

A non-profit partnership. A 60/40 cash split. A legacy at the end.

BCH eligibility requires NFP sponsorship. The structure is designed so the building serves the community in perpetuity — and rewards the owner who took the risk to build it.

Annual Cash Flow Waterfall

1 · Net Operating Income100%
2 · Less Annual Mortgage (BCH MLI)$522K
3 · Net Income After Debt ServiceSplit 60/40
→ Owner / Investor60%
→ NFP Partner40%

Clarification: the 60/40 split applies only to net cash flow after mortgage/debt service. It does not split gross revenue or NOI before debt.

At Year 20 · The Legacy Event

The BCH forgivable is fully forgiven. The mortgage is largely paid down. The owner exits with their cumulative 60% cash flow plus the mortgage paydown — and the building itself remains as a permanent community asset stewarded by the NFP.

Why This Structure Exists

Build Canada Homes was designed for buildings exactly like this.

Small-format, mission-aligned residential assets in growing-but-not-urban Ontario towns sit in a structural financing gap.

01

Too small for institutional debt

$22M total cost is below the threshold most senior-housing REITs and pension funds will underwrite as a single asset.

02

Too NOI-thin for conventional 30-year amort

Stabilized NOI of $1.14M can't service market-rate 30-year debt on the construction cost it requires.

03

Exactly the asset class BCH targets

Affordable housing for vulnerable populations (seniors), in mid-sized Ontario communities, with NFP alignment and municipal partnership. The program was built for this.

Risks & Considerations

What we are still verifying.

BCH Eligibility

Program qualification not yet confirmed

BCH 40% forgivable component requires NFP sponsor, deep affordability covenant, and program approval. Mitigation: structure NFP partnership early; engage CMHC and BCH at concept stage; secure municipal letter of support.

Total Project Cost

$22M is a working assumption

Detailed QS estimate required. Boutique senior living in small-market Ontario typically prices $750–$950/SF all-in. Mitigation: lock GMP procurement; value-engineer non-resident-facing scope.

Forgiveness Mechanics

Forgivable timeline is program-dependent

Returns model assumes BCH forgivable portion remains forgiven through 5-yr hold. If forgiveness vests over 20 years, early sale may trigger partial repayment.

Lease-Up Pace

Year 1 BCH DCR is 0.91×

Lease-up reserve required. Pro forma Year 1 occupancy of 82% must be defended with referral pipeline (physicians, hospital discharge planners, local financial advisors).

Affordability Covenant

BCH may require below-market unit set-aside

If 30%+ of units must be rented at affordable rates, blended rent of $4,580/mo will compress. Mitigation: model affordable variant with 30% discount on a portion of suites.

Regulatory Path

RHRA licensing locks operating model

Licensed Independent-Supportive Living requires 2+ regulated services (meals + medication oversight). Mitigation: confirm path before construction docs; build clinical contracts in pre-opening.
