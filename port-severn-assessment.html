01 The Situation — Why K0RE Is at the Table
⚠ Court Order / Distressed Lender Position
The current owner/borrower at 74 Honey Harbour Drive is operating under a court order and cannot satisfy obligations to the first-position lender (K0RE or K0RE-affiliated). An immediate capital solution of approximately $5.5M is required to discharge or restructure the first charge. Without intervention the project faces receivership or forced sale — destroying long-term value for everyone.
📌 What's Already In Place
This is not a blank-sheet deal. A full BCH submission package was prepared (Cover Memo April 25, 2026) under Ubuntu Land Trust & Developments as NFP sponsor, with Cube Building Systems (Brampton volumetric modular factory) as construction partner. An executed landowner MOU is on file. The BCH application is live or near-submission. K0RE's intervention saves this structure — it doesn't restart from scratch.

The Opportunity: K0RE steps in as bridge capital provider, takes control of the deal structure, brings in the builder partner as equity co-participant, and carries the deal forward through the BCH/CMHC stack. In return, K0RE and the builder partner capture the project economics across a 5–10 year horizon that includes:

  • Developer/sponsor fees across 416 units (~$68.4M total BCH grant deployed)
  • 60% NOI participation during the 20-year affordability period via Ubuntu LTD ground lease
  • Year-20 land repurchase event at formula price ($16.64M original land value + inflation)
  • Construction volume for the builder: 416 modular units over 4 phases = significant backlog fill
  • First-lender recovery on the K0RE-held charge

06 Risk Assessment
Court Order / Lender Default Risk

Highest-priority risk. Every day without resolution increases the chance of receivership or forced sale that voids the BCH application. K0RE's bridge capital is the only mitigant. Speed of execution is critical.

Builder Partner Commitment Risk

The entire bridge raise hinges on the builder's LOI. Without it, bridge lenders can't see a construction-ready project. This is Cal's immediate action item. Once the LOI is in hand, everything else sequences cleanly.

BCH Application Timing Risk

The "Construction Start <12 Months" designation is time-sensitive. Losing it through delay or receivership would set back Phase 1 approval materially. The Cover Memo is dated April 25, 2026 — file immediately if not already done.

Construction / Cost Risk

Cube's fixed-price contracts, performance bonds, and ±3% variance history effectively mitigate this. Lowest construction risk profile available in Canadian affordable housing at this scale.

Underwriting / DSCR Risk

1.59x DSCR is 27% above the 1.25x target and 45% above the 1.10x hard floor. Meaningful headroom under stress. $2,150 blended AMR is not aggressive for the Muskoka rural market. Same DSCR holds clean across all 4 phases.

Land / Phasing Risk

Sequential appraisals eliminate double-pledging concern. Phases 2–4 are structurally insulated from Phase 1 — each stands on its own re-appraisal and prior-phase operating data. If any re-appraisal fails, earlier BCH commitments are protected.

Year-20 Refinancing Risk

If credit markets are unavailable at Year-20, the contractual extension activates: lease extends 5–10 years, landowner distributions suspend (accrue Prime+2%), non-profit retains 100% of NOI to build reserves. CMHC mortgage fully amortizes by Year 25 regardless.

Municipal / Approvals Risk

Township of Georgian Bay is a supportive partner. DC waiver ($3.09M) is part of the Phase 1 stack — needs written confirmation as part of close, but the relationship is established through Ubuntu LTD's landowner MOU.
