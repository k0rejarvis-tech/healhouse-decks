01 The Situation — Why K0RE Is at the Table
⚠ Court Order / Distressed Lender Position
The current owner/borrower at 74 Honey Harbour Drive is operating under a court order and cannot satisfy obligations to the first-position lender (K0RE or K0RE-affiliated). An immediate capital solution of approximately $5.5M is required to discharge or restructure the first charge. Without intervention the project faces receivership or forced sale — destroying long-term value for all parties.
📌 What's Already In Place — This Is Not a Restart
A full BCH submission package was prepared (Cover Memo April 25, 2026) under Ubuntu Land Trust & Developments as NFP sponsor, with Cube Building Systems (Brampton volumetric modular) as construction partner. An executed landowner MOU is on file. The BCH application is live or near-submission. K0RE's intervention saves this structure — it takes it over and pushes it forward.

K0RE's Play: K0RE steps in as bridge capital provider, assumes project control through Ubuntu LTD sponsorship, brings in a builder-partner as equity co-participant, and carries the deal forward through the BCH/CMHC stack. The existing owner remains in the structure but in a subordinated recovery position — treated fairly, not exploited. In return, K0RE and the builder partner capture the project economics across a 5–10 year horizon:

  • Developer/sponsor fees across 416 units (~$68.4M total BCH grant deployed)
  • 60% NOI participation during the 20-year affordability period via Ubuntu LTD ground lease
  • Year-20 land repurchase event at formula price ($16.64M original land value + inflation)
  • Construction volume for the builder: 416 modular units over 4 phases
  • First-lender recovery + origination fee on the K0RE bridge

07 Risk Assessment
Court Order / Lender Default Risk

Highest-priority risk. Every day without resolution increases receivership/forced-sale risk that voids the BCH application. K0RE's bridge capital is the only mitigant. Speed of execution is critical.

Builder Partner Commitment Risk

The bridge raise hinges on the builder's LOI. Without it, bridge lenders see distressed land. With it, they see a $171M construction-ready deal. Cal's Week 1 action item.

BCH Timing Risk

"Construction Start <12 Months" designation is time-sensitive. Losing it through delay could push Phase 1 approval back by a full program cycle. Cover Memo is April 25, 2026 — file immediately if not done.

Construction / Cost Risk

Cube's fixed-price contracts, ±3% variance history, performance bonds, and factory model effectively eliminate traditional construction overrun risk. Lowest-risk delivery method available.

Underwriting / DSCR Risk

1.59x DSCR is 27% above target and 45% above hard floor. Meaningful stress headroom. $2,150 blended AMR is not aggressive for the Muskoka corridor. Same numbers hold clean across all 4 phases.

Land / Phasing Risk

Sequential appraisals eliminate double-pledging concern. Each phase stands on its own re-appraisal and prior-phase operating data. Earlier BCH commitments are fully insulated from later-phase assumptions.

Existing Owner Litigation Risk

If owner feels squeezed out unfairly, they could challenge the deal structure, creating delay. Mitigant: the capital recovery note and ongoing NOI participation keep them whole — removing the incentive to litigate.

Municipal Approvals Risk

Township of Georgian Bay is supportive. DC waiver ($3.09M) needs written confirmation — existing Ubuntu LTD relationship makes this achievable. Target confirmation within 30 days of bridge close.
