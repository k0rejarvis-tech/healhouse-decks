Crew is the wedge. Supersize is the sale. Operators are the moat.

K0RE should go to market as a premium AI creation and execution platform: members bring an idea, opportunity, business problem, property, workflow, product, or campaign; Crew turns it into an executive-grade artifact; K0RE converts the best ones into paid operator-led implementation sprints.

Market read

Why now

AI interest is huge, scaling is weak

McKinsey’s 2025 AI survey says most companies are still experimenting or piloting rather than scaling AI across the enterprise.

Agents are becoming table stakes

Gartner predicts task-specific agents will move from under 5% of enterprise apps in 2025 to 40% by end of 2026.

Execution gap = K0RE opening

McKinsey has also framed the gap plainly: many organizations experiment with gen AI, but fewer than 10% have scaled agents.

Positioning

K0RE’s category

The AI command center for turning ideas, opportunities, and operations into built assets.

Not “another chatbot.” Not “AI productivity.” K0RE is closer to an operator layer: assessment, artifact, scope, sprint, delivery, memory, and improvement loop.

One-liner

Homepage message

Bring K0RE an idea. Leave with a board-ready plan — or a build sprint underway.

  • For founders validating products.
  • For real estate and investment teams assessing opportunities.
  • For businesses turning messy workflows into automation.
  • For creators and operators packaging offers, campaigns, and assets.

ICP 1

Founders & creators

They have ideas, offers, audiences, half-built products, and messy execution. Crew gives instant clarity; Supersize gives implementation.

  • Hooks: “Validate your idea in 10 minutes.”
  • Artifacts: app blueprint, pitch deck, GTM pack, sprint scope.
  • Paid path: Build sprint / MVP sprint / launch pack.
ICP 2

Real estate / investment operators

They need fast diligence, memo creation, risk framing, investor-ready narratives, and repeatable deal workflows.

  • Hooks: “Turn a deal into an investor-grade memo.”
  • Artifacts: diligence memo, risk controls, deck, capital narrative.
  • Paid path: Strategy Pack / investor deck / underwriting workflow.
90-day launch plan

GTM sequence

Days 1–30: Proof + demos

  • Create 10 flagship artifacts across product, real estate, GTM, automation.
  • Record short demo videos: idea → artifact → Supersize.
  • Launch landing page around Crew.
  • Use founder/operator outreach to book 10 pilot calls.

Days 31–60: Paid pilots

  • Sell 5–10 Strategy Packs.
  • Convert 2–3 into Build Sprints.
  • Collect before/after case studies.
  • Tighten package pricing and scope templates.

Days 61–90: Scale wedge

  • Publish case studies and artifact gallery examples.
  • Add referral loop: “Share your artifact.”
  • Launch niche pages: AI business plan, real estate memo, app blueprint, workflow automation.
  • Start outbound to agencies, investors, RE operators, founders.

Website changes

What k0re.ai should say

  • Lead with Crew and artifacts.
  • Show 4 use-case cards: Product, Real Estate, Marketing, Automation.

Success metrics

What to measure

  • Visitor → Crew run conversion.
  • Crew run → saved workspace conversion.
  • Saved artifact → Share with K0RE conversion.
  • Submission → scoped/priced conversion.
  • Priced → paid sprint conversion.
  • Average sprint value and delivery margin.
Recommendation

The sharp GTM call

Do not launch K0RE as a giant everything-platform. Launch Crew as the premium front door, then use K0RE’s deeper memory, operators, command center, and mobile app as the reason customers stay.

This keeps the promise understandable: “K0RE helps me turn things I’m thinking about into things that are assessed, packaged, scoped, and built.” That is much easier to sell than “personal AI operating system” on day one.
