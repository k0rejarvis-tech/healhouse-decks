Green P into affordable housing.

A short internal deck to help colleagues get on side.

Heal House is asking the City to support a pathway for the Green P parcel to become part of a broader affordable housing and community-wellness solution, delivered with a non-profit partner and clear public-benefit protections.

The Opportunity

A parking parcel can unlock a deeper civic outcome.

The Green P lands sit inside a larger Lakeshore opportunity. On their own, they serve a narrow civic function. As part of the Heal House assembly, they can become affordable housing, wellness infrastructure, and a permanent neighbourhood benefit.

Today: limited use as a standalone municipal parcel.
Unlocked: part of a complete site plan with housing and services.
Outcome: public land value converted into visible community benefit.

The Proposal

Heal House secures Green P. The City secures affordable housing outcomes.

This is a public-benefit proposal. The Green P transfer, sale, or land-swap-equivalent should be structured around the affordable housing outcome Toronto wants delivered — with Heal House assembling the site and a non-profit partner stewarding affordability.

The Land Exchange Logic

A land swap in spirit: public land value exchanged for affordable housing delivery.

The City can treat Green P as a public-benefit lever. Rather than viewing the parcel only as a parking asset, staff can frame it as a contribution toward affordable housing, wellness services, and a stronger complete-community outcome.

City

Defines the land pathway and public-benefit conditions.

Heal House

Secures the parcel and integrates it into the complete-site plan.

Non-profit

Delivers and stewards the affordable housing commitment.

Land value → public benefit06 / 08

Public Protections

The City can make land available without losing accountability.

Possible safeguards

Affordable housing covenant registered against the project.
Non-profit role documented in the delivery structure.
Public-benefit reporting back to the City.
Milestones tied to planning and delivery progress.

What this avoids

No City operating burden.
No complicated ownership partnership.
No unnecessary complexity in the first conversation.
No dilution of the core public outcome.
Accountability without complexity07 / 08
