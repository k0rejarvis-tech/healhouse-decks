Eligibility Window

  • Agreement of Purchase and Sale: After March 31, 2026 AND before April 1, 2027
  • For rental use: Construction must be substantially completed by December 31, 2029
  • For primary residence: Construction begins by Dec 3, 2028, completed by Dec 31, 2031
  • Applies to: Corporations AND individuals (no first-time buyer restriction)
  • Eligible properties: Residential condo units (single-unit residential complex)
  • Bill status: Bill 114 introduced May 5, 2026 (first reading); federal regulations pending
⚠️ Critical Caveat — Tax Payable at Closing
Until CRA forms and federal regulations are finalized, buyers must pay the full HST at closing and claim the rebate directly from CRA afterward. Do NOT rely on the builder crediting the rebate at closing yet — builders cannot do this until federal forms are released. Plan for cash flow to cover the HST upfront.

For faster closings and more flexible underwriting: 1st & 2nd mortgages, bridge loans, up to 80% LTV. Rates typically prime + 2-4%. Good for time-sensitive bulk deals where the discount window is narrow.

Execution Process — Step by Step

  1. Identify target building — Work with commercial broker (CBRE, Colliers, C&W) for off-market bulk deals from developers. Target buildings with 20%+ unsold inventory. One building only (single status cert, single reserve fund).
  2. Verify eligibility — Ensure AGPS between April 1, 2026 and March 31, 2027. Confirm construction started (builder must show this). Verify unit types (avoid "buried bedroom" layouts).
  3. Secure financing — Portfolio mortgage through broker for all 5-10 units. Or private lender for speed. Plan for cash to cover HST at closing (rebate comes later from CRA).
  4. Close the purchase — Pay full HST (13%) at closing — do NOT count on builder crediting it yet. Confirm Tarion warranty status, status certs for all units, and reserve fund health.
  5. Claim the rebate — File Enhanced NRRPR (New Residential Rental Property Rebate) with CRA after forms are released. Rebate flows directly to investor within weeks.
  6. Lease immediately — Generate cash flow to service debt. Average Toronto studio rents start ~$1,600/month, larger units up to $3,000/month. Average ~$2,000-2,300. New builds = not under rent control.
  7. Hold 3-5 years — Market rebound thesis: zero new condo starts in Q1 2026, supply cliff arriving in 2-3 years. Both Jesta and High Art target 5-year hold before resale.
  8. Exit — Sell to qualified individual or institutional buyer when market recovers. Debt paydown + appreciation = primary return drivers.

Key Risks & Red Flags

Market Risks

  • Developer sells bulk units below market → drags down values for existing owners (and future exit comps)
  • Over-30% investor concentration in one building can affect future resale marketability
  • CRA may challenge rental use designation if units sit vacant >6 months (Vacant Home Tax applies)
  • HST rebate forms/regulations still pending — cash flow gap at closing could be significant ($455K-$910K depending on deal size)

Financing Risks

  • Banks may cap investor mortgage portfolios if investor already owns 4+ rental properties
  • Interest rate exposure on variable-rate portfolio mortgages
  • CMHC MLI Select has affordability/energy efficiency requirements
  • Private lender rates (prime + 2-4%) eat into margins significantly

Bottom Line — The Play

The thesis: Buy 5-10 units in one building from a developer with unsold inventory, close with HST rebate eligibility, leverage portfolio financing, rent immediately for cash flow, hold 3-5 years until the supply cliff hits (no new starts in Q1 2026), then exit at higher comps.

The margin: 20% bulk discount + 13% HST rebate on reduced price = ~30.3% total effective reduction off list price. On a $700K unit, that's ~$212,800 saved — turning a $700K price tag into a $487K effective cost.

The window: AGPS must be signed April 1, 2026 – March 31, 2027. Federal CRA forms still pending — plan for cash to cover HST at closing. ~50 private funds already active; competition will increase as rebate forms are released.
