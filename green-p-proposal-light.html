City of Toronto Staff Briefing

Green P into affordable housing.

A staff-facing version for circulation.

Heal House is asking the City to support a pathway for the Green P parcel to become part of a broader affordable housing and community-wellness solution — delivered with a non-profit partner, clear public-benefit protections, and a stronger long-term revenue story.

The Opportunity

A parking parcel can unlock a deeper civic outcome.

The Green P lands sit inside a larger Lakeshore opportunity. On their own, they serve a narrow civic function. As part of the Heal House assembly, they can become affordable housing, wellness infrastructure, and a permanent neighbourhood benefit.

Today: limited use as a standalone municipal parcel.
Unlocked: part of a complete site plan with housing and services.
Outcome: underused land converted into long-term municipal revenue and visible social benefit.

The Proposal

A Green P pathway can convert public land value into measurable affordable housing outcomes.

This is a public-benefit proposal. The Green P transfer, lease, disposition, or partnership pathway should be evaluated through the affordable housing outcome Toronto wants delivered — with Heal House assembling the site and a non-profit partner stewarding affordability.

Housing-first framingGreen P pathwayNon-profit deliveryCity public benefit

This briefing does not ask staff to pre-approve a transaction. It asks staff to identify the appropriate City pathway for evaluating whether Green P can be converted into a measurable affordable housing and community-benefit outcome.

Why It Fits Toronto

The City gets mission-aligned housing without becoming the developer.

Toronto priorities

More affordable homes, stronger long-term property-tax revenue, public land value converted into public benefit, and community-serving uses at grade.

Heal House role

Lead the site vision, coordinate the land pathway, and partner with a non-profit to deliver affordability.

City priorities aligned08 / 11

The Land Exchange Logic

A public-benefit land transaction: public land value converted into affordable housing delivery.

The City can treat Green P as both a public-benefit and revenue lever. Rather than viewing the parcel only as a parking asset, staff can evaluate the appropriate process for converting underused land into affordable housing, wellness services, long-term tax generation, and a stronger complete-community outcome.

City

Defines the land pathway and public-benefit conditions.

Heal House

Secures the parcel and integrates it into the complete-site plan.

Non-profit

Delivers and stewards the affordable housing commitment.

Public Protections

The City can make land available without losing accountability.

Possible safeguards

Affordable housing covenant registered against the project.
Non-profit role documented in the delivery structure.
Public-benefit reporting back to the City.
Milestones tied to planning and delivery progress.

What this avoids

No City operating burden.
No complicated ownership partnership.
No unnecessary complexity in the first conversation.
No dilution of the core public outcome.

The Short Story

Toronto can turn a parking parcel into affordable homes.

The ask is simple: create the City pathway for Heal House to integrate Green P into the complete site, then deliver affordability through a non-profit partner with clear public-benefit protections.
