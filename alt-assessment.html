⚠ What You Lose Without Green P

  • $6M in free land — gone. Every dollar must be financed or equity-funded.
  • 130 fewer units — significantly reduced revenue base and NOI.
  • No hotel revenue — loses the high-yield diversified income stream.
  • Weaker forgivable loan position — smaller project may not qualify for the same 40% level.
  • Reduced investor bump — less land to refinance, smaller equity creation at close.
  • Higher cost per unit — smaller buildings lose economies of scale.
Assessment Conclusion

Highest & Best Use Recommendation

The Full Deal with Green P is Significantly Superior

The alternative scenario produces a viable development — but it delivers substantially less value on every metric. The Green P contribution is a force multiplier that unlocks scale, hotel revenue, stronger government funding eligibility, and a more compelling investor story.

However, if Green P Falls Through:

  • 2304 (60 units) is the stronger standalone play — larger lot, more density, lower cost per unit
  • 2294 (30 units) works as a smaller infill project but has tight margins at 6 floors on a narrow lot
  • Combined (90 units) still represents a meaningful mid-rise development on Lakeshore — but without the hotel or Green P, the non-profit structure loses much of its advantage
  • Consider pursuing the $6M new lot acquisition regardless — it pairs well with 2304 even without Green P
Bottom Line: Get the Green P. The entire deal economics shift dramatically in your favor. Without it, you're building a decent mid-rise. With it, you're building a legacy.
