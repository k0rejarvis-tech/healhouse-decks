BCH Common Ground Initiative — Legal Briefing
Re: 2008 Brimley Road, Toronto (Scarborough), ON
Purpose of This Brief
This document summarizes the proposed transaction structure for 2008 Brimley Road under the Common
Ground Initiative — a non-profit affordable housing model utilizing Build Canada Homes (BCH) federal grant
funding and CMHC MLI Select mortgage insurance. It is intended to orient your client's legal counsel to the deal
architecture in advance of definitive documentation.
The structure is materially different from a conventional land sale and warrants careful review of the documents
that govern the long-term relationship between the landowner and the non-profit operating entity.
Transaction Overview
Rather than transferring fee simple title to the non-profit at closing, the landowner contributes the land as
equity into a single-purpose entity that develops and operates a purpose-built rental project. The landowner
receives staged cash payments and a 60% share of project net cash flow over a 20-year term, with a guaranteed
buyout at Year 20.

Documents Your Client's Counsel Will Review
The transaction will be governed by a coordinated package of agreements. The principal documents are:
•
Purchase & Sale Agreement (PSA): Conditional on BCH approval and CMHC commitment. Provides for
Day 1 cash payment at first construction draw and Year 20 final payment.
•
Land Lease / Ground Lease: 99-year lease subordinated to the CMHC mortgage. Provides Ubuntu LTD
operational control of the building from Day 1 while landowner retains beneficial interest in the land
until Year 20.
•
Joint Venture / Operating Agreement: Governs the 60/40 net cash flow split, distribution mechanics,
reporting obligations, and reserve requirements.
•
CMHC Loan & Security Documentation: Standard CMHC MLI Select forms. Mortgage holds first-position
security; ground lease is subordinated. CMHC has non-disturbance rights.
•
BCH Funding Agreement: Governs the 40% federal grant including affordability covenants, reporting,
and forgiveness terms over the 20-year vesting period.
•
Step-In Rights & Default Cure Provisions: Reciprocal step-in rights between landowner and Ubuntu LTD.
Landowner can cure defaults, assume operations, or replace the operator (subject to CMHC approval) if
material covenants are breached.
Landowner Protections — Highlights for Counsel
Specific provisions that materially de-risk the structure for the landowner:
•
Personal guarantee release at stabilization. Personal guarantees provided during construction are
automatically released upon project stabilization (90% occupancy + 1.15× DSCR for 3 consecutive
months). Typical timeline: 24–36 months from closing.
•
Construction risk allocated to project, not landowner. Fixed-price modular construction contracts with
performance bonds and liquidated damages. Cost overruns flow to the contractor and bonding
company, not the landowner.

•
Year 20 buyout is contractually obligated. Ubuntu LTD has a contractual obligation to acquire the land
at Year 20 at the formula-based price. The vested BCH grant ($4.94M) plus mortgage paydown funds
this purchase.
•
Refinancing risk is structurally mitigated. If credit markets are unavailable at Year 20, an automatic
ground lease extension activates (5–10 years). Landowner distributions accrue interest at prime + 2%
during the extension. The land purchase is then funded from accumulated operations.
•
Affordability covenants are enforceable against both parties. Both Ubuntu LTD and the landowner are
bound by the affordability covenants. Government enforcement rights apply throughout the 20-year
term.
•
DSCR-triggered cash flow waterfall protection. If DSCR falls below 1.15× for two consecutive quarters,
landowner net cash flow share automatically increases from 60% to 80% until reserves are replenished.
Tax Considerations & Market Context
Counsel should review with the client's tax advisor:
•
The property is the landowner's principal residence and qualifies for the principal residence exemption
(PRE), meaning a conventional sale would generate no capital gains tax.
•
Current market conditions value a cash sale of this property in the range of $1,200,000 – $1,600,000. A
vendor take-back (VTB) financing structure may stretch the headline price to ~$2,000,000, but the VTB
carries buyer-default risk and the seller becomes a creditor for the deferred portion.
•
Under the Common Ground structure, the landowner receives $1,400,000 cash at first construction
draw — equal to or greater than a strong cash sale outcome — without exposure to a buyer's payment
performance.
•
The principal residence is contributed to the project at the start of construction. Counsel should review
whether the principal residence designation can be preserved through year of contribution, and how the
change-in-use rules (subsection 45(2) ITA election) may apply to defer or eliminate any deemed
disposition.
•
The 60% net cash flow distributions to the landowner over 20 years are passive rental income, taxed at
the landowner's marginal rate. This is a different tax profile than the lump-sum sale proceeds.
•
If structured through a holdco or family trust, additional planning options around income splitting and
estate planning become available — worth a separate consultation.
Note: Ubuntu LTD does not provide tax advice. The above is informational only. Counsel and the landowner's tax advisor
should structure for optimal tax outcomes given the principal residence status.

Questions Your Counsel Will Likely Ask
•
How is the Year 20 buyout price determined? Formula-based on the original landowner equity
contribution ($600,000), not market value at Year 20. This protects against speculative inflation but also
caps upside.
•
What happens if Ubuntu LTD becomes insolvent or fails to perform? Landowner step-in rights allow
assumption of operations or replacement of operator (subject to CMHC approval). CMHC also has
independent step-in rights as senior lender.
•
Can the landowner sell or assign their interest before Year 20? Subject to non-profit and CMHC
consent. Estate succession and corporate restructuring are permitted; speculative resale is contractually
prohibited.
•
What is the personal guarantee exposure? Limited to construction phase (~24–36 months). Released
automatically upon project stabilization criteria (90% occupancy + 1.15× DSCR for 3 consecutive
months).
•
What is the affordability covenant's practical impact? Minimum 30% of units priced below 80% CMHC
Median Market Rent. The remaining 70% can be at market rents. The blended economics are reflected
in the underwriting.
•
What if BCH funding is not approved? LOI and PSA are conditional on BCH approval. If declined, the deal
does not proceed and there is no obligation on either side. Ubuntu LTD bears the application costs.
